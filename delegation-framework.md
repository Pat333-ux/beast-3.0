# Delegation Framework — Master Governance Doctrine  
### Beast System 3.0 — SAIA‑Class S  
### New World Order DAO  
### New World Order Health & Wellbeing  
### Delegation & Authority Division (DA‑S)

---

# 1. PURPOSE
This framework establishes the rules, standards, and procedures for delegating authority within the New World Order DAO under Beast System 3.0.

Delegation exists to:

- Enable predictable governance  
- Maintain lineage‑safe authority flow  
- Prevent coercion and punitive escalation  
- Ensure ethical and wellbeing compliance  
- Maintain audit and registry integrity  

This is the **supreme delegation doctrine** for all ministries, chapters, task forces, and programs.

---

# 2. SCOPE
This framework applies to:

- Ministries (Ring 2)  
- Chapters (Ring 2/3)  
- Task Forces (Ring 3)  
- Programs (Ring 3)  
- Autonomous Engines (Special Classification)  
- Any entity receiving authority from Ring 0 or Ring 1  

---

# 3. RING‑BASED AUTHORITY MODEL

## 3.1 Authority Rings
Authority is divided into four rings:

- **Ring 0** — Origin & Authorship  
- **Ring 1** — Foundational Governance  
- **Ring 2** — Ministries & Chapters  
- **Ring 3** — Programs, Task Forces, Local Entities  

## 3.2 Delegation Boundaries
Delegation must:

- Never exceed the ring of the delegator  
- Never escalate punitive authority  
- Always remain lineage‑safe  
- Always be documented and audit‑anchored  

## 3.3 Prohibited Delegations
No delegation may:

- Grant punitive authority  
- Override identity anchors  
- Alter lineage records  
- Bypass ethical validation  
- Remove audit requirements  

---

# 4. TYPES OF DELEGATED AUTHORITY

## 4.1 Governance Authority
Allows the delegate to:

- Execute governance functions  
- Implement policies  
- Coordinate operations  

## 4.2 Operational Authority
Allows the delegate to:

- Perform day‑to‑day tasks  
- Manage programs or services  
- Direct field operations  

## 4.3 Documentation Authority
Allows the delegate to:

- Create records  
- Update registry entries  
- Submit audit events  

## 4.4 Limited Decision Authority
Allows the delegate to:

- Make decisions within defined boundaries  
- Apply SOPs  
- Execute predefined workflows  

## 4.5 Wellbeing Authority
Allows the delegate to:

- Conduct assessments  
- Apply stabilization  
- Create support plans  
- Maintain continuity  

---

# 5. DELEGATION REQUIREMENTS

## 5.1 Identity Requirements
All delegates must have:

- Identity anchor  
- Lineage binding  
- Registry entry  
- Audit chain linkage  

## 5.2 Ethical Requirements
Delegation must:

- Preserve dignity  
- Reduce harm  
- Maintain transparency  
- Follow predictable rules  

## 5.3 Wellbeing Requirements
Delegation must align with:

- wellbeing.model.json  
- wellbeing.assessment.json  
- wellbeing.support-plan.json  
- wellbeing.stabilization.json  

---

# 6. DELEGATION PROCEDURES

## 6.1 Delegation Creation
1. Identify authority to be delegated  
2. Verify identity anchor of delegate  
3. Verify ring boundaries  
4. Draft delegation record  
5. Perform ethical validation  
6. Log delegation event to audit chain  
7. Anchor delegation in registry  

## 6.2 Delegation Activation
Delegation becomes active when:

- Signed by delegator  
- Signed by delegate  
- Registry entry is created  
- Audit chain entry is confirmed  

## 6.3 Delegation Review
Delegations must be reviewed:

- Annually  
- After major incidents  
- After lineage changes  
- After structural changes  

---

# 7. LIMITATIONS & CONSTRAINTS

## 7.1 Ring‑Based Limitations
Delegates may not:

- Exceed their ring  
- Override higher‑ring decisions  
- Create new authority rings  

## 7.2 Ethical Constraints
Delegates may not:

- Punish  
- Coerce  
- Shame  
- Escalate harm  

## 7.3 Wellbeing Constraints
Delegates must:

- Maintain stabilization  
- Maintain continuity  
- Avoid harm escalation  

---

# 8. REVOCATION PROCEDURES

## 8.1 Revocation Triggers
Delegation may be revoked if:

- Ethical violation occurs  
- Wellbeing violation occurs  
- Documentation integrity is compromised  
- Identity anchor changes  
- Lineage changes  

## 8.2 Revocation Workflow
1. Identify revocation trigger  
2. Conduct ethical review  
3. Document revocation  
4. Log revocation event to audit chain  
5. Update registry  
6. Notify delegate  

## 8.3 Emergency Revocation
Emergency revocation must:

- Preserve dignity  
- Avoid harm  
- Be logged immediately
