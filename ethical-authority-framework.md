# Ethical Authority Framework  
### Beast System 3.0 — SAIA‑Class S  
### New World Order DAO  
### New World Order Health & Wellbeing  

---

## 1. Purpose of the Ethical Authority Framework
The Ethical Authority Framework defines the principles, constraints, and decision‑making logic that govern all actions within Beast System 3.0.  
It ensures that every exercise of authority is:

- Ethical  
- Predictable  
- Trauma‑preventive  
- Dignity‑first  
- Transparent  
- Documented  

This framework forms the core of the Ethical Authority Layer (EA‑S).

---

## 2. Foundational Ethical Principles

### **2.1 Dignity as a Non‑Negotiable Invariant**
Every person must be treated with inherent dignity.  
No action may:

- Humiliate  
- Degrade  
- Coerce  
- Dehumanize  

Dignity is the highest constraint in the system.

---

### **2.2 Harm‑Reduction as a Primary Directive**
All decisions must reduce harm, not increase it.  
If an action introduces risk, the system must:

- Pause  
- Reassess  
- Apply harm‑reduction logic  
- Choose a safer alternative  

Harm‑reduction overrides procedural convenience.

---

### **2.3 Ethical Predictability**
Ethical authority must be:

- Deterministic  
- Documented  
- Consistent  
- Transparent  

Unpredictable authority is considered harmful.

---

### **2.4 Non‑Punitive Governance**
Punitive actions are not default responses.  
Supportive pathways must always be considered first.

Punishment is only permissible when:

- All supportive options are exhausted  
- Ethical checks are passed  
- Dignity invariants remain intact  

---

## 3. Ethical Decision Architecture (EA‑S)

The Ethical Authority Layer operates through a structured decision model:

### **3.1 Input Event**
A situation, request, or condition enters the system.

### **3.2 Context Evaluation**
The system evaluates:

- Lived experience  
- Environmental factors  
- Emotional state  
- Safety indicators  

### **3.3 Dignity Check**
If dignity is threatened → **STOP**.

### **3.4 Harm Check**
If harm is likely → **STOP**.

### **3.5 Ethical Path Selection**
The system selects the least harmful, most supportive pathway.

### **3.6 Outcome Validation**
The system verifies:

- Dignity preserved  
- Harm reduced  
- Predictability maintained  
- Documentation complete  

---

## 4. Ethical Constraints on Authority

### **4.1 Ring‑Based Authority Limits**
Authority must follow the SAIA‑Class S ring hierarchy:

- Ring 0 → Origin Authority  
- Ring 1 → Foundational Authority  
- Ring 2 → Delegated Authority  
- Ring 3 → Operational Authority  

No authority may exceed its ring classification.

---

### **4.2 No Reverse Authority Flow**
Authority cannot flow upward.  
Lower rings cannot override higher rings.

---

### **4.3 No Lateral Escalation**
Authority cannot be transferred sideways without explicit authorization.

---

### **4.4 Ethical Override**
Ethical constraints override:

- Procedural rules  
- Operational shortcuts  
- Delegated authority  
- Ministry‑level decisions  

If an action violates dignity or increases harm, it is invalid.

## 6. Ethical Documentation Requirements

All ethical decisions must be:

- Logged in `ethical-decision-log.json`  
- Chained in `ethical-audit-chain.json`  
- Linked to wellbeing assessments  
- Linked to identity and registry records  

No ethical log may be deleted or altered.
