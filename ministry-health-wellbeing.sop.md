# Ministry of Health & Wellbeing  
## Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)  
### Beast System 3.0 — SAIA‑Class S  
### New World Order Health & Wellbeing  
### Ministry Operations Division (MHW‑SOP)

---

# 1. PURPOSE
This SOP establishes the operational procedures, standards, workflows, and documentation requirements for all wellbeing services delivered by the Ministry of Health & Wellbeing.

It ensures that all ministry operations:

- Preserve dignity  
- Prevent trauma  
- Maintain predictable, non‑punitive governance  
- Follow the 2026 Wellbeing Standard  
- Align with identity and lineage anchors  
- Meet federal‑context documentation rigor  

This SOP is binding on all ministry personnel, chapters, programs, and task forces.

---

# 2. SCOPE
This SOP applies to:

- Wellbeing assessments  
- Stabilization services  
- Support planning  
- Continuity and follow‑up  
- Harm‑prevention interventions  
- Community wellbeing programs  
- Family stabilization services  
- Crisis‑avoidance workflows  
- Documentation and registry operations  
- Audit chain operations  

---

# 3. GOVERNANCE & AUTHORITY

## 3.1 Ring‑Based Authority
The Ministry operates under:

- **Ring 1** — Foundational Wellbeing Authority  
- **Ring 2** — Ministry Operations  
- **Ring 3** — Programs, Chapters, Task Forces  

Authority must always:

- Flow predictably  
- Respect ring boundaries  
- Be documented  
- Be lineage‑safe  

## 3.2 Delegated Authority
Delegation must:

- Be explicit  
- Be documented  
- Pass ethical validation  
- Follow wellbeing constraints  

---

# 4. WELLBEING SERVICE WORKFLOWS

## 4.1 Assessment Workflow
1. Receive wellbeing signal  
2. Verify identity anchor  
3. Conduct assessment using:  
   - wellbeing.assessment.json  
   - wellbeing.model.json  
4. Document findings  
5. Log event to audit chain  
6. Update registry  

## 4.2 Stabilization Workflow
1. Identify stabilization need  
2. Apply stabilization protocol  
3. Ensure dignity‑first interaction  
4. Document stabilization actions  
5. Log event to audit chain  

## 4.3 Support Plan Workflow
1. Create support plan using:  
   - wellbeing.support-plan.json  
2. Validate plan ethically  
3. Review with participant  
4. Document plan  
5. Update registry  

## 4.4 Continuity Workflow
1. Schedule follow‑ups  
2. Maintain continuity logs  
3. Document outcomes  
4. Update registry  
5. Log to audit chain  

## 4.5 Harm‑Prevention Workflow
1. Identify harm‑risk indicators  
2. Apply harm‑prevention protocol  
3. Document actions  
4. Log event  
5. Notify oversight body if required  

---

# 5. ETHICAL & WELLBEING REQUIREMENTS

## 5.1 Ethical Invariants
All ministry actions must:

- Preserve dignity  
- Reduce harm  
- Maintain transparency  
- Follow predictable rules  

## 5.2 Prohibited Actions
The Ministry may not:

- Punish  
- Coerce  
- Shame  
- Escalate harm  
- Override identity anchors  
- Alter lineage records  

## 5.3 Ethical Validation
Required for:

- Identity changes  
- Lineage changes  
- Support plan creation  
- Stabilization overrides  
- Documentation overrides  

---

# 6. DOCUMENTATION PROCEDURES

## 6.1 Documentation Requirements
All records must be:

- Immutable  
- Version‑controlled  
- Registry‑anchored  
- Audit‑chained  

## 6.2 Required Documents
- Assessment record  
- Stabilization log  
- Support plan  
- Continuity log  
- Harm‑prevention record  
- Registry update  
- Audit event  

## 6.3 Documentation Integrity
No document may be:

- Deleted  
- Altered without versioning  
- Removed from the audit chain  

---

# 7. AUDIT PROCEDURES

## 7.1 Audit Chain Requirements
Every event must include:

- Event ID  
- Timestamp  
- Actor ID  
- Hash  
- Previous hash  
- Context  
- Payload  

## 7.2 Chain Validation
Audit chains must be:

- Validated  
- Immutable  
- Linked  
- Complete  

## 7.3 Corrective Action
Corrective actions must be:

- Non‑punitive  
- Documented  
- Audit‑anchored
