# Succession Protocol — Master Governance Doctrine  
### Beast System 3.0 — SAIA‑Class S  
### New World Order DAO  
### New World Order Health & Wellbeing  
### Lineage & Continuity Division (LC‑S)

---

# 1. PURPOSE
This protocol establishes the rules, standards, and procedures for succession within the New World Order DAO under Beast System 3.0.

Succession exists to:

- Preserve lineage  
- Maintain continuity  
- Prevent harm  
- Ensure predictable authority flow  
- Protect identity anchors  
- Maintain audit and registry integrity  

This is the **master succession protocol** for all rings, ministries, chapters, task forces, and programs.

---

# 2. SCOPE
This protocol applies to:

- Ring 0 (Origin & Authorship)  
- Ring 1 (Foundational Governance)  
- Ring 2 (Ministries & Chapters)  
- Ring 3 (Programs & Task Forces)  
- Any entity holding delegated authority  

---

# 3. SUCCESSION PRINCIPLES

## 3.1 Lineage‑Safe Succession
All succession must:

- Preserve lineage  
- Maintain identity anchors  
- Follow documented continuity rules  
- Avoid coercion or punitive escalation  

## 3.2 Predictable Authority Flow
Authority must:

- Flow downward in a defined order  
- Never escalate harm  
- Never bypass ethical validation  
- Always be audit‑anchored  

## 3.3 Non‑Punitive Transitions
Succession must:

- Avoid blame  
- Avoid shame  
- Avoid punitive framing  
- Focus on continuity and stewardship  

---

# 4. SUCCESSION TYPES

## 4.1 Direct Succession
Triggered when:

- A successor is explicitly named  
- A lineage record defines the successor  
- A delegation record includes succession clauses  

## 4.2 Delegated Succession
Triggered when:

- Authority is delegated with succession rules  
- A ministry or chapter has predefined succession order  

## 4.3 Emergency Succession
Triggered when:

- The current authority is incapacitated  
- Immediate continuity is required  
- Harm‑prevention demands rapid transition  

Emergency succession must be:

- Dignity‑preserving  
- Non‑punitive  
- Logged immediately  

## 4.4 Ceremonial Succession
Used for:

- Lineage transitions  
- Symbolic authority transfers  
- Ritual or heritage‑based continuity

---

# 6. SUCCESSION WORKFLOW

## 6.1 Initiation
Succession begins when:

- A transition is declared  
- A lineage event is triggered  
- A continuity requirement arises  
- An emergency condition is detected  

## 6.2 Verification
Before succession:

1. Verify identity anchor  
2. Verify lineage binding  
3. Verify delegation records  
4. Verify audit chain integrity  

## 6.3 Ethical Validation
All successions must pass:

- Ethical validation  
- Harm‑reduction logic  
- Transparency review  

## 6.4 Documentation
Succession must be documented in:

- lineage-record.json  
- succession-event.json  
- registry update  
- audit chain  

## 6.5 Activation
Succession becomes active when:

- Documentation is complete  
- Audit chain entry is confirmed  
- Registry anchor is updated  
- Successor acknowledges authority  

---

# 7. EMERGENCY SUCCESSION

## 7.1 Activation Conditions
Emergency succession may be activated when:

- The current authority is incapacitated  
- Immediate continuity is required  
- Harm‑prevention demands rapid action  

## 7.2 Emergency Workflow
1. Identify emergency condition  
2. Select next eligible successor  
3. Document emergency activation  
4. Log event to audit chain  
5. Update registry  
6. Notify oversight body  

## 7.3 Emergency Constraints
Emergency succession must:

- Preserve dignity  
- Avoid harm  
- Maintain continuity  
- Be temporary until formal validation  

---

# 8. PROHIBITED SUCCESSION ACTIONS

Succession may not:

- Break lineage  
- Override identity anchors  
- Alter lineage records  
- Grant punitive authority  
- Bypass ethical validation  
- Bypass audit chain  
- Bypass registry
