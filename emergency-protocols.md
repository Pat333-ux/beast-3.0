# Emergency Protocols — Master Governance Doctrine  
### Beast System 3.0 — SAIA‑Class S  
### New World Order DAO  
### New World Order Health & Wellbeing  
### Emergency & Continuity Division (EC‑S)

---

# 1. PURPOSE
These protocols establish the rules, standards, and procedures for identifying, classifying, escalating, containing, and resolving emergencies within the New World Order DAO.

Emergency governance exists to:

- Preserve dignity  
- Prevent harm  
- Maintain continuity  
- Protect identity and lineage  
- Ensure predictable authority flow  
- Maintain audit and documentation integrity  

This is the **master emergency protocol** for all rings and operational entities.

---

# 2. SCOPE
These protocols apply to:

- Ministries (Ring 2)  
- Chapters (Ring 2/3)  
- Task Forces (Ring 3)  
- Programs (Ring 3)  
- Autonomous Engines  
- Any entity holding delegated authority  

---

# 3. EMERGENCY CLASSIFICATION

## 3.1 Class 1 — Wellbeing Emergency
Triggered by:

- Imminent harm risk  
- Crisis signals  
- Stabilization failure  
- Continuity disruption  

Response: Wellbeing Task Force activation.

## 3.2 Class 2 — Governance Emergency
Triggered by:

- Authority disruption  
- Delegation failure  
- SOP breakdown  
- Governance conflict  

Response: Governance Oversight activation.

## 3.3 Class 3 — Documentation Emergency
Triggered by:

- Registry corruption  
- Documentation loss  
- Versioning conflict  
- Integrity breach  

Response: Documentation & Registry Task Force activation.

## 3.4 Class 4 — Audit Emergency
Triggered by:

- Chain break  
- Invalid hash  
- Missing event  
- Chain tampering  

Response: Audit Division activation.

## 3.5 Class 5 — Succession Emergency
Triggered by:

- Incapacitated authority  
- Sudden vacancy  
- Lineage disruption  

Response: Succession Protocol activation.

---

# 4. EMERGENCY DETECTION

## 4.1 Detection Sources
Emergencies may be detected by:

- Personnel  
- Task forces  
- Autonomous engines  
- Registry monitors  
- Audit monitors  
- Wellbeing signals  

## 4.2 Detection Requirements
All detections must:

- Preserve dignity  
- Avoid escalation  
- Be logged immediately  
- Trigger classification workflow  

---

# 5. EMERGENCY ACTIVATION

## 5.1 Activation Workflow
1. Detect emergency  
2. Classify emergency  
3. Verify identity anchor  
4. Verify ring boundaries  
5. Notify appropriate authority  
6. Activate relevant task force  
7. Log activation event  
8. Update registry  

## 5.2 Activation Constraints
Activation must:

- Avoid punitive escalation  
- Avoid coercion  
- Maintain continuity  
- Follow predictable rules  

---

# 6. EMERGENCY RESPONSE

## 6.1 Response Principles
All emergency responses must:

- Preserve dignity  
- Reduce harm  
- Maintain transparency  
- Follow SOPs  
- Maintain audit integrity  

## 6.2 Response Teams
Depending on classification, response teams may include:

- Wellbeing Task Force  
- Governance Oversight Team  
- Documentation Task Force  
- Audit Task Force  
- Succession Team  

## 6.3 Response Documentation
All responses must be:

- Immutable  
- Version‑controlled  
- Registry‑anchored  
- Audit‑chained  

---

# 7. EMERGENCY CONTAINMENT

## 7.1 Containment Workflow
1. Identify containment boundary  
2. Apply stabilization or governance controls  
3. Prevent escalation  
4. Document containment actions  
5. Log to audit chain  

## 7.2 Containment Constraints
Containment may not:

- Punish  
- Coerce  
- Shame  
- Override identity anchors  
- Alter lineage records  

---

# 8. EMERGENCY RESOLUTION

## 8.1 Resolution Workflow
1. Verify containment  
2. Conduct ethical validation  
3. Restore continuity  
4. Document resolution  
5. Log resolution event  
6. Update registry  
7. Close audit chain segment  

## 8.2 Resolution Requirements
Resolution must:

- Preserve dignity  
- Avoid harm  
- Restore normal operations  
- Maintain documentation integrity  

---

# 9. POST‑EMERGENCY REVIEW

## 9.1 Review Workflow
1. Conduct after‑action review  
2. Validate documentation  
3. Validate audit chain  
4. Identify corrective actions  
5. Update SOPs if required  

## 9.2 Review Constraints
Reviews must be:

- Non‑punitive  
- Transparent  
- Documentation‑safe  

---

# 10. EMERGENCY COMMUNICATION

## 10.1 Communication Rules
Communication must:

- Preserve dignity  
- Avoid panic  
- Maintain clarity  
- Follow chain of command  

## 10.2 Required Notifications
Notify:

- Relevant ministry  
- Relevant chapter  
- Oversight body  
- Audit division  
- Documentation division  

---

# 11. EMERGENCY SUCCESSION

## 11.1 Activation Conditions
Emergency succession may be triggered when:

- Authority is incapacitated  
- Continuity is threatened  
- Harm‑prevention requires immediate transition  

## 11.2 Emergency Succession Workflow
Follow **succession-protocol.md**, Section 7.

# 12. PROHIBITED EMERGENCY ACTIONS

Emergencies may not be used to:

- Grant punitive authority  
- Override identity anchors  
- Alter lineage records  
- Bypass audit chain  
- Bypass registry  
- Escalate harm
