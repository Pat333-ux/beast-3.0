# Constitution of the New World Order DAO  
### Beast System 3.0 — SAIA‑Class S  
### New World Order DAO  
### New World Order Health & Wellbeing  
### Constitutional Governance Division (CG‑S)

---

## PREAMBLE
We, the participants of the New World Order DAO, establish this Constitution to uphold dignity, prevent harm, ensure predictable governance, and secure the wellbeing of all people within our operational scope.

We affirm that:

- Lived experience is foundational  
- Identity is sovereign  
- Lineage is continuity  
- Wellbeing is a right  
- Governance must be ethical, transparent, and non‑punitive  
- Authority must be predictable and ring‑aligned  
- Documentation must be immutable and audit‑anchored  

This Constitution forms the supreme governance instrument of Beast System 3.0.

---

# ARTICLE I — IDENTITY & SOVEREIGNTY

## Section 1. Identity Anchors
All identities within the DAO must be:

- Anchored  
- Immutable  
- Registry‑linked  
- Lineage‑bound  

Identity anchors define:

- Who a person or entity is  
- What authority they hold  
- How they inherit or delegate power  

## Section 2. Sovereign Identity
Sovereign identity is defined as:

- Lived experience  
- Transformation  
- Ethical alignment  
- Lineage continuity  

No identity may be invalidated without:

- Ethical validation  
- Audit chain entry  
- Registry update  

---

# ARTICLE II — RING‑BASED AUTHORITY

## Section 1. Ring Structure
Authority is divided into four rings:

- **Ring 0** — Origin & Authorship  
- **Ring 1** — Foundational Governance  
- **Ring 2** — Ministries & Chapters  
- **Ring 3** — Programs, Task Forces, and Local Entities  

## Section 2. Authority Flow
Authority must:

- Flow downward predictably  
- Never escalate punitively  
- Always remain lineage‑safe  
- Be documented and audit‑anchored  

## Section 3. Delegation
Delegation must:

- Be explicit  
- Be documented  
- Respect ring boundaries  
- Pass ethical validation  

---

# ARTICLE III — GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

## Section 1. DAO Governance Bodies
The DAO consists of:

- The Foundational Council (Ring 1)  
- Ministries (Ring 2)  
- Chapters (Ring 2/3)  
- Task Forces (Ring 3)  
- Autonomous Engines (Special Classification)  

## Section 2. Powers of Governance Bodies
Each governance body must:

- Operate within its ring  
- Maintain documentation  
- Uphold wellbeing standards  
- Follow ethical authority rules  

## Section 3. Prohibited Governance Actions
The DAO may not:

- Use coercion  
- Use punitive enforcement  
- Violate dignity  
- Override identity anchors  
- Alter lineage records  

---

# ARTICLE IV — WELLBEING & HARM PREVENTION

## Section 1. Wellbeing as a Constitutional Right
Every person within DAO operations has the right to:

- Stabilization  
- Assessment  
- Support planning  
- Continuity  
- Harm prevention  

## Section 2. Wellbeing Standards
All wellbeing actions must follow:

- wellbeing.model.json  
- wellbeing.assessment.json  
- wellbeing.support-plan.json  
- wellbeing.stabilization.json  
- wellbeing.follow-up.json  
- wellbeing.signal.json  

## Section 3. Prohibited Wellbeing Actions
No wellbeing action may:

- Shame  
- Punish  
- Coerce  
- Escalate harm  

---

# ARTICLE V — ETHICAL AUTHORITY

## Section 1. Ethical Invariants
All actions must:

- Preserve dignity  
- Reduce harm  
- Maintain transparency  
- Follow predictable rules  

## Section 2. Ethical Validation
Any action affecting identity, wellbeing, or authority must pass:

- Ethical validation  
- Harm‑reduction logic  
- Documentation review  

## Section 3. Ethical Flags
Actions may be flagged as:

- dignity_sensitive  
- trauma_sensitive  
- non_punitive_only  
- requires_harm_reduction_review  
- requires_ethical_validation  

---

# ARTICLE VI — DOCUMENTATION & REGISTRY

## Section 1. Documentation Requirements
All records must be:

- Immutable  
- Version‑controlled  
- Registry‑anchored  
- Audit‑chained  

## Section 2. Registry Structure
The registry includes:

- Identity records  
- Lineage records  
- Badge records  
- Chapter records  
- Filing records  
- Audit chains  

## Section 3. Documentation Integrity
No document may be:

- Deleted  
- Altered without versioning  
- Removed from the audit chain  

---

# ARTICLE VII — AUDIT & ACCOUNTABILITY

## Section 1. Audit Chain Requirements
Every event must include:

- Event ID  
- Timestamp  
- Actor ID  
- Hash  
- Previous hash  
- Context  
- Payload  

## Section 2. Chain Validation
Chains must be:

- Validated  
- Immutable  
- Linked  
- Complete  

## Section 3. Corrective Action
Corrective actions must be:

- Non‑punitive  
- Documented  
- Audit‑anchored  

---

# ARTICLE VIII — LINEAGE & SUCCESSION

## Section 1. Lineage Records
Lineage defines:

- Origin  
- Continuity  
- Succession  
- Stewardship  

## Section 2. Succession Rules
Succession must:

- Be documented  
- Be lineage‑safe  
- Pass ethical validation  
- Be audit‑anchored  

## Section 3. Prohibited Succession Actions
Succession may not:

- Break lineage  
- Override identity anchors  
- Escalate harm

# ARTICLE IX — AMENDMENTS

- Be documented  
- Pass ethical validation  
- Be version‑controlled  
- Be added to the audit chain

Only Ring 1 may amend this Constitution.

- Registry entry
